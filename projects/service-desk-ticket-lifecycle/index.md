## Ticketing Approach

<details>
<summary><strong>Core Principles (Click to expand)</strong></summary>

<div class="approach-section">

### Triage & Classification
- Focus on **scope, impact, and service restoration** before deep technical investigation  
- Determine if the issue is **isolated or widespread**, and whether it's a complete blocker  
- Classify as **incident** (unplanned disruption) or **service request** (standard fulfilment)

### Prioritisation
- Assess **impact**: How many users? Are critical tasks blocked?  
- Assess **urgency**: Can work continue? Is it convenience or productivity?  
- Prioritise high-impact issues affecting multiple users or time-sensitive operations

### Troubleshooting
- Start with **common causes and simple fixes** â€” connectivity, physical connections, basic config  
- Keep investigation **scoped appropriately** â€” isolated issues stay local; widespread symptoms get escalated  
- Rule out before escalating; document what's been tried

### Escalation
Escalate when:
- Resolution cannot be completed confidently or within reasonable timeframes  
- Backend systems, policies, or controlled processes are involved  
- Indicators suggest a wider underlying issue  
- Responsibility resides with a specialist or designated team

### Documentation & Closure
- Capture **issue description, actions taken, factors ruled out, and outcome**  
- Write notes so another technician can pick up without duplicating work  
- Close only after **service restoration is confirmed** with the user

</details>

## Ticket Structure

Each ticket follows a consistent format designed for clarity and handover:

| Section | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| **Scenario** | What the user reported â€” the starting point |
| **Areas Assessed** | ITIL practices and focus areas guiding triage |
| **Ticket Details** | Impact, urgency, users affected, workaround availability, priority |
| **Troubleshooting** | Steps taken, factors ruled out, findings |
| **Resolution / Escalation** | Outcome and next actions |
| **Closure Notes** | Confirmation of service restoration, knowledge captured |
